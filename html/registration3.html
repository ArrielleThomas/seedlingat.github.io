Code of Conduct

At Seedling Theater Company, we respect childhood. We are adults for most of our lives and the world is filled of influences that are pushing our kids to grow up too quickly. Our studio is committed to fostering an enviornment where those influences are checked at the door, where kids feel safe, and where they are not asked to compromise personal values. Our code of conduct is an agreement between our staff, you, and your children.
Seedling Theater Company promises to do our best to show respect for our students by:
  • Using appropriate language.
  • Managing student behavior with positvity and patience.
  • Selecting only costumes, music, and dramatic materials that are age appropriate.
  • Keeping the lines of communication open and addressing the concerns of students and parents.
  • Maintaining a culture of kindness, support, and encouragement in all our classes.
    • In return, each of our students are expected to:
  • Using appropriate language.
  • Managing student behavior with positvity and patience.
  • Selecting only costumes, music, and dramatic materials that are age appropriate.
  • Keeping the lines of communication open and addressing the concerns of students and parents.
  • Maintaining a culture of kindness, support, and encouragement in all our classes.
    • Dress appropriately, avoiding:
  • Short-shorts and skirts
  • Midriff baring tops.
  • Strapless or spaghetti strapped tops.
  • Anything printed with offensive words or images.
    • Treat with care and respect the property of Seedling Theater Company including:
  • Microphones and other audio equipments.
  • Costumes.
  • Stage curtains.
  • Sets.
  • Props.
  • Furniture.
  • The building.
    • Additionally, parents and or guardians are asked to please:
  • Refrain from profainity and crudness in speech while in the building.
  • Abide by the safety rules and regulations as well as your children.
  • Pick up your child on time.
  • Understand that what your child learns in each class is more important than the role they're given in a show.
  • Communicate with teachers and management ANY concerns that may arise from participation in our programs.
